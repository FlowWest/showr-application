Welcome to Showr

The decision support tool for Shasta Reservoir operations for Winter Run Chinook Salmon management

Showr is a data-driven web application designed to help decision makers and interested stakeholders understand and engage in the complicated process of managing Shasta Reservoir operations to protect Winter Run Chinook Salmon. Development of showr (make this consistent with title bar) began in 2017 and has advanced through iterative, collaborative effort between fisheries and water management experts involved in Winter Run Chinook Salmon management (U.S. Bureau of Reclamation, the National Marine Fisheries Service, the California Department of Fish and Wildlife, the State Water Resources Control Board, Sacramento River Settlement Contractors, University of Washington Columbia Basin Research Program) and a team of data scientists and web developers at FlowWest. The design and functionality of Showr was presented at monthly technical committee meetings and then updated based on user feedback between each meeting. Showr is currently maintained by the Sacramento River Settlement Contractors and is integrated with the SacPAS web application and the Reclamation Information Sharing Environment (RISE).

Using Showr

Management of Shasta Reservoir to protect Winter Run Chinook Salmon is driven by the Reasonable and Prudent Actions (RPAs) detailed in the 2009 Biological Opinion on the Long-Term Operations of the Central Valley Project and State Water Project. The Winter Run Chinook Salmon RPAs are primarily focused on cold water management in Shasta Reservoir, temperature thresholds in the Sacramento River downstream of Shasta, and Winter Run Chinook Salmon adult spawning, egg survival, and fry emergence. To address these conditions, Showr provides real-time data on Shasta Reservoir storage and cold water pool conditions, Sacramento River water temperature and flow conditions, and Winter Run Chinook Salmon monitoring. The Showr development team recommends that users start on the Dashboard page for a snapshot summary of existing conditions across all of these aspects of Shasta Management for Winter Run Chinook Salmon, and then proceed to the Temperature, Flow, and Winter Run Chinook pages for a deeper dive into individual management conditions.
